Summary Statement

Velociraptor (/vəˌlɒsɪˈræptər, vəˈlɒsɪræptər/;[1] lit. 'swift thief') is a genus of small dromaeosaurid dinosaurs that
lived in Asia during the Late Cretaceous epoch, about 75 million to 71 million years ago. Two species are
currently recognized, although others have been assigned in the past. The type species is V. mongoliensis,
named and described in 1924. Fossils of this species have been discovered in the Djadochta Formation,
Mongolia. A second species, V. osmolskae, was named in 2008 for skull material from the Bayan Mandahu
Formation, China.
